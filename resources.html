Implicit Bias

Here are resources that can help measure your own implicit bias. Implicit bias is defined as a negative attitude, of which one is not consciously aware, against a specific social group. We often have our own internal judgements of people based on our own experiences, meaning that avoiding the utilization of implicit bias is difficult, but not impossible. It plays into the concept of intersectionality as well since historic prejudices held against groups of people can shape their own biases against said people for years to come, effectively ravaging any hope of building a civil environment for all.

The Harvard Implicit Bias Tests contains a comprehensive list of surveys that help measure your own implicit bias. When we took the tests, we were often surprised by how quickly we were tripped up by the tests' many curveballs. The link is below.
